Write a simple poem

In this activity you will learn how to write a poem inspired by pictures.

Using images – prints, portraits, photographs, even objects – can be a wonderfully effective source of inspiration for writing poems. In this activity you will write a poem about yourself using images as a trigger.

Use these images to write a self-portrait, a short poem that describes something about yourself in a light hearted way.

This course was created by - The Poetry Society Visit their website: www.poetrysociety.org.uk

Choose your images

Choose 3 images by tapping – do not to think about it too long, but to go for those that catch your attention. Use your finger to swipe down to find more images.

Example

Each line should begin with the words “I’ll tell you who I am...”, or a variation of this. The example below uses ‘goat’, ‘box’ and ‘bath’ from the image bank.

I’ll tell you who I am I’m always acting the goat, according to my Dad.
I’ll tell you who I am: A box filled with chocolate and laughter and secrets.
I’ll tell you who I am: I’m trouble, is who I am, I’m always in hot water.
